
Earnings by Education Level (USBureauLabor.csv)
What it is: Official U.S. government data showing how much people earn depending on education.
Data you can extract: Median weekly earnings for bachelor’s vs. master’s degree holders — can convert to annual salaries.
https://www.bls.gov/emp/tables/unemployment-earnings-education.htm

Notes:
Shows median weekly earnings and unemployment rates by highest education level.
Useful for estimating average earnings difference between bachelor’s and master’s grads.
Values are for all fields, but still a strong national baseline.

Average Starting Salaries for Master’s (BadCredit.org)
What it is: Summary of average starting salaries for master’s degree holders—including CS.
Data you can extract:
Median starting salary for CS & related high-paying majors ~ $105,890.
https://www.badcredit.org/studies/average-student-loan-debt-by-degree/

Notes:
Gives estimated starting pay by major area including computer and information sciences.
Great source to build your salary table for master’s vs bachelor’s, especially for CS specifically.

Average Graduate Student Loan Debt (NerdWallet)
What it is: Published overview of average debt for graduate degrees in the U.S.
Data you can extract:
Average student loan debt for master’s degree holders ~ $69,140.
Graduate students overall average ~ $88,220 when including undergrad debt.
https://www.nerdwallet.com/student-loans/learn/average-student-loan-debt-graduate-school

Notes:
Offers a breakdown between graduate debt and total debt including undergrad.
Useful for estimating the cost side of grad school.

Field of Degree Earnings (Census)
What it is: Government data showing earnings by major field.
Data you can extract:
CS degree holders earn median ~$100,000 or more annually.
https://www.census.gov/newsroom/press-releases/2023/field-of-degree-earnings.html

Notes:
Great source for average earnings specific to computer science vs other fields.
You can use this as a more targeted salary dataset than BLS overall.
